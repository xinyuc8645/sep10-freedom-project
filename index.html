What Is Animation? And Who Discovered It?

What exactly is animation? Animation is where you are able to make a block still image turn into an image that can move with various moves. For example, let’s say there’s a picture of a person standing near the door. But we want to make that person do some kind of movement and wave goodbye to someone. Thus, this is where animation comes in clutch and can help you make those movements come in real.

Animations were first discovered in the 19th century, more specifically in 1907 in France by an artist named “Emile Cohl.” He discovered animation during his career in the industry of filmmaking using the advantage of stop motion techniques. Throughout the years, many inventions have been made in order prior to animation, but how does modern technology come in usage in animation?

Well, modern technologies help to create the animations of certain characters and their motions more life-like, the environments realistic like the winds blowing, and having certain action sequences to the characters. Both modern and old animation technologies will be discussed below.
